ORIGAMI CAMEL

INTERESTING FACTS ABOUT CAMELS

  • Camels can completely shut their nostrils during sandstorms
  • There are over 160 words for camel in Arabic alone.
  • Mother camels carry their calves up to 14 months before giving birth.
  • Camels have thick lips which let them forage for thorny plants other animals can’t eat.
  • Camels have three sets of eyelids and two rows of eyelashes to keep sand out of their eyes.

ORIGAMI CHAMELEON

  • Almost half of all known species live in Madagascar
  • They change color in order to communicate, regulate body temperature and hide themselves from predators
  • They have skin crystals which enable them to change color at will
  • Their eyes can swivel around in two different directions simultaneously
  • Smaller chameleons have faster tongues

ORIGAMI PANDA

  • Giant pandas are good at climbing trees and can also swim.
  • Pandas have so many fans because they look cute.
  • Pandas are "lazy" — eating and sleeping make their day.
  • An adult can eat 12–38 kilos of bamboo per day
  • Pandas have carnivorous teeth, but they eat bamboo and fruit.

ORIGAMI PIGEON

  • Pigeons can fly at altitudes up to and beyond 6000 feet, and at an average speed of 77.6 mph.
  • They can detect sounds at far lower frequencies than humans are able to, and can thus hear distant storms and volcanoes.
  • Pigeons are fed by many members of different religions including Muslims, Hindus and Sikhs for spiritual reasons.
  • Pigeons have excellent hearing abilities.
  • They can detect sounds at far lower frequencies than humans are able to, and can thus hear distant storms and volcanoes.

ORIGAMI TEDDY-BEAR

  • Teddy bears got their name from the story that Teddy Roosevelt refused to shoot a bear cub while on a hunting trip in 1902.
  • The first toy stuffed bear was created by German toymaker Margarete Steiff. She created a soft plush with movable arms and legs and a friendly face after taking her nephew to the zoo in 1903.
  • The world's smallest stitched teddy bear is a mere 0.29 inches tall! It was created by South African artist Cheryl Moss who is known for her minuscule work.
  • The official term for a teddy bear collector is an ‘arctophile’.
  • he Oxford English Dictionary dates the first use of the term teddy bear to 1906.

ORIGAMI FLYING-CICADA

  • Cicadas improve lawns by digging tunnels that allow air into the soil.
  • They’re true bugs (from the order Hemiptera). So you can call them a bug and be scientifically accurate.
  • The loud whirring or buzzing sound you hear is an all-male cicada chorus.
  • Females make a much quieter sound by flicking their wings
  • Most have red-orange eyes. But occasionally cicadas have blue, white or grayish eyes
